What is Thalassemia?

Thalassemia is a genetic blood disorder where patients are unable to produce enough hemoglobin. They require regular blood transfusions for survival.

Why Blood Donation Matters

  • Patients need transfusions every 2–4 weeks.
  • Each unit of blood extends a patient’s life.
  • Your donation provides hope and health.

How You Can Help

Donate blood regularly or help patients by sharing their request forms to reach more donors.
